Who Procrastinates and How to Fix It

Almost everyone procrastinates, and there is nothing to be ashamed of. However, we want to help you overcome this habit to increase success.

Likely, anyone you know has, at some point or another, procrastinated. The easiest fix is to develop motivation and incentivize yourself to get something done by rewarding yourself.

Some possible rewards may include:

  • A treat or snack
  • A break from the task
  • Release of stress
  • A good grade on an assignment

However, to stop procrastinating, you must know that you are procrastinating and why you do it.

According to the McGraw Center at Princeton University, acquiring insight into how procrastination protects you from feeling like “not enough” and remembering it when tempted to procrastinate, you are more likely to stop procrastinating.

To help stop procrastination

Some other tips include:

  • Begin an assignment as soon as it is assigned
  • Break it into smaller chuncks for easier management
  • Identify times you want to work and remove distractions
  • Continue to remind yourself of the goal
  • Move away from or remove any external distractions

For the majority of people, they have to move their electronics away from their workspace to get work done. Some people work better when they sit in a quiet place away from loud noises or other people who could distract them. If you work better in a quiet place but still need some white noise, a fan or music can be an option for background sound.

Sometimes, different forms of lighting can help with focus, so you might want to adjust the lights in your workspace to your liking. If you can't focus near technology, only keep what you need (like a computer, clock, or device for noise). A common strategy is to place your devices in a separate room or lock them in a drawer far away so you have to move out of your study area to get them.
